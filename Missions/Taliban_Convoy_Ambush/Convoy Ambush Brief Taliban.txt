Desperate Measures

---

Date:
November 14th, 2001
1800

---

Situation Report:
We (the Taliban) are ambushing an armed American supply convoy. 
The targets are supply trucks. 
The targets are to be captured intact, or in such a state that they can be repaired in the field.
The objective is to get the trucks to the mosque in Bastam, and bring them inside the walls.
Once inside, the Americans should not fire on us, unless fired upon first.
If we can't secure those supplies, it's better that nobody has them.

---

Intelligence:
5 - 15 vehicles in the convoy
Past convoy routes have been scouted and recorded without any variation, however we shouldn't push our luck. Don't give the Americans any reason to change their route.

We should expect to have available:
	various transport trucks
	a few truck-mounted machine guns
	one truck-mounted spg

Based on surveillance and patrol patterns, we're expecting the convoy to have some mounted machine guns, and maybe an IFV or two.

Due to the proximity of an American-held airbase, air cover for the convoy is possible.
However as the conflict in this region has cooled down in the last weeks, we aren't expecting them to have heavily armed CAS ready; however, they certainly have it.

As the Americans are unprepared for an ambush, it's likely the convoy is rather spread out. From past experience, the spacing between convoy elements is approximately 100m - 350m.

If the Americans get word to their airbase or FOB to the North, we can expect enemy reinforcements and possibly a QRF. If that happens, time will be of the essence.

Further, the Takistani National Guard (TNG), while officially allied with the Americans against us, has many of our supporters ready to help us or defect. We might get lucky and get their support, or they might denounce us.
Further, local elements of the TNG might or might not be hostile towards us, depending on the commanding officer. If we try to hide our guns, they might think we're civilians, or the CO might be a supporter. 
In Feruz Abad, we have many supporters, but at the TNG Airbase to the south, we can expect them to open fire on us. 

---

Diary Entry:
American forces have pushed back the Taliban front lines. We've been hiding in some mines near Feruz Abad.
Our air supply routes have been cut off, and our dwindling stockpile won't last forever. 

Covert supporters of our cause within the Takistani National Guard (TNG) and National Police Force have been supplying us with arms, however the TNG have started to notice the missing supplies. 
Our current situation is unsustainable. 

Our short term goal is to gather enough resources to attack and subsequently gain control of a supply route, any supply route.

Taliban loyal elements within the TNG have informed us of a large supply shipment landed 7 hours ago at the Northern American-held airport. 

Our sources say the TNG hasn't been able to field much armour or air assets due to fuel shortages. The Americans control the oil. 
It's only a matter of time before the Americans send a supply convoy to their allies.

We've managed to scout the route that previous American supply convoys have taken. We're hoping that this one takes the same route. 

The plan is simple: Ambush the convoy, and then take the supplies and vehicles to the mosque in Bastam. The Americans aren't allowed in a religious site, unless we fire first. 

---

Technical Notes:
Asset respawns: none
Infantry respawns: zeus' discretion, but only as defectors of the TNG.
Therefore platoon needs to therefore pick one uniform for Taliban, and one uniform for defectors of TNG.
It would be smart to get two loadouts ready. 

Restrictions:
zeus reserves the right to restrict NVGs (probably no NVGs)

Because the TNG (officially blufor) has been supplying the Taliban with arms, you may take blufor rifles, at platoon's and squad lead's discretion (no blufor launchers).

Any vehicle in the convoy or in the town of Falar can be commandeered with platoon's approval. Platoon may delegate this to squad leads at platoon's discretion. 
TNG defectors (respawns) may take police offroads where they start.
